# Petrichor

## What It Is

Petrichor is the distinctive, pleasant smell that comes from earth after rain, especially after a long dry period. It's that clean, fresh scent that makes you want to breathe deeply when rain begins to fall on hot pavement or dry soil. The aroma is created by oils secreted by plants during dry periods, which are released into the air when moistened by rain, combined with a chemical called geosmin produced by soil bacteria.

## Origin

The term was coined in 1964 by Australian researchers Isabel Joy Bear and Richard Thomas, combining the Greek words "petros" (stone) and "ichor" (the fluid that flows in the veins of the gods in Greek mythology). They were studying the atmospheric chemistry behind this universally recognized but previously unnamed scent.

The researchers discovered that petrichor results from oils produced by plants during dry weather, which get absorbed by clay-based soils and rocks. When it rains, these oils are released along with geosmin, creating the characteristic earthy smell that humans find almost universally pleasant.
